<p><p>The seaweed fiber market is categorized into two main types: staple and filament. Staple fibers are short lengths of seaweed fibers, typically processed into various products like textiles and insulation materials. They offer versatility and are often blended with other fibers. Filament fibers, on the other hand, are long, continuous strands and are primarily used in applications requiring strength and flexibility, such as ropes and nets. Both types reflect the growing demand for sustainable materials in various industries.</p></p>
